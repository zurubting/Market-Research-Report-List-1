<p><p>The electric modular grippers market primarily features two main types: parallel grippers and angular grippers. Parallel grippers operate by moving their gripping fingers in a parallel motion, making them ideal for gripping flat objects with consistent grip force. Angular grippers, on the other hand, utilize a pivoting motion to clamp objects at an angle, offering flexibility for gripping irregularly shaped items. Both types are widely used in automation and robotics industries due to their efficiency and adaptability in various applications.</p></p>
