<p><p>The polymerization initiator market is categorized into several types, including persulfates, peroxides, azo compounds, and others. Persulfates, such as ammonium and potassium persulfate, are often used for their thermal stability and efficiency in producing free radicals. Peroxides, like benzoyl peroxide, are favored for their rapid initiation at elevated temperatures. Azo compounds release nitrogen-based free radicals upon decomposition, making them effective initiators in various polymerizations. The "others" category encompasses alternative initiators, including UV photoinitiators and redox systems, expanding the market's versatility.</p></p>
