<p><p>The gasoline engine market is primarily divided into two types: 2-stroke and 4-stroke engines. 2-stroke engines complete a power cycle in two strokes of the piston, making them lighter and simpler, often used in small applications like chainsaws and mopeds. In contrast, 4-stroke engines execute the cycle in four strokes, providing better fuel efficiency, lower emissions, and greater power, commonly found in cars and motorcycles. The choice between them depends on specific needs, such as performance or efficiency.</p></p>
