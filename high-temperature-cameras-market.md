<p><p>High temperature cameras are specialized imaging devices designed to operate efficiently in extreme thermal environments. The market primarily includes two types of sensors: CCD (Charge-Coupled Device) and CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor). CCD sensors are known for their high image quality and low noise levels, making them suitable for applications demanding precision. In contrast, CMOS sensors offer faster processing speeds and lower power consumption, making them ideal for real-time monitoring and cost-effective solutions. Together, these technologies cater to various industrial and scientific needs.</p></p>
