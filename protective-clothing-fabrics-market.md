<p><p>The protective clothing fabrics market is primarily divided into two types: inherent fabrics and treated fabrics. Inherent fabrics are made from fibers that provide permanent protection against hazards, such as flame or chemical exposure, without requiring special treatments. Treated fabrics, on the other hand, are standard materials enhanced through chemical treatments or coatings to improve their protective properties. Each type offers distinct advantages, with inherent fabrics delivering reliability and durability, while treated fabrics can provide versatility and cost-effectiveness for various applications.</p></p>
