<p><p>Occupancy monitoring involves various sensor technologies to detect presence in spaces. Microwave sensors use electromagnetic waves to sense movement and presence, offering long-range detection. Infrared Time-of-Flight sensors measure distance by calculating the time it takes for infrared light to reflect off a person, providing precise occupancy data. Ultrasonic sensors emit sound waves to detect motion within a defined area. Other markets may include visual sensors or passive infrared sensors, each with unique applications and advantages in occupancy detection and analysis.</p></p>
