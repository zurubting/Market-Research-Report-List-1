<p><p>The organic pigment dispersions market primarily comprises water-based and oil-based types. Water-based dispersions use water as a solvent, offering eco-friendly properties, low VOC emissions, and ease of cleaning. They are popular in applications such as paints, coatings, and textiles. Conversely, oil-based dispersions utilize organic solvents, providing enhanced durability and adhesion, making them suitable for applications requiring robust performance, like industrial coatings and printing inks. Each type serves distinct applications based on environmental considerations and performance requirements.</p></p>
