<p><ul><li>Prick</li><li>Intradermal</li><li>Patch</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Allergy skin tests are essential for diagnosing allergic reactions and are categorized into three primary types: prick, intradermal, and patch tests. Prick tests involve placing allergens on the skin's surface and making small punctures to assess reactions. Intradermal tests inject allergens just beneath the skin to detect more subtle sensitivities. Patch tests evaluate delayed allergic responses by applying allergens to patch devices on the skin for extended periods. Together, these methods provide comprehensive insights into individual allergies and sensitivities.</p></p>
