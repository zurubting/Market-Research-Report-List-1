<p><ul><li>Air Breathing Engines</li><li>Non-Air Breathing Engines</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Flight propulsion systems can be categorized into two main types: air-breathing engines and non-air-breathing engines. Air-breathing engines, such as turbojets and turbofans, utilize atmospheric oxygen for combustion, making them ideal for powered flight within Earth's atmosphere. In contrast, non-air-breathing engines, like rocket engines, carry both fuel and oxidizer, enabling propulsion in space or environments lacking atmospheric oxygen. Each type serves distinct applications, with air-breathing engines primarily suited for commercial and military aviation, while non-air-breathing engines are essential for space exploration and missions beyond Earth's atmosphere.</p></p>
